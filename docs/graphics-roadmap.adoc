= Graphics Roadmap
:toc:

Modernization of the SimpliCity graphics: motivation, how the pipeline
works, what has been done, and what is still TODO.

== Motivation

The original MicropolisJ art dates back to 1980s hardware limits: a
~14-color palette with heavy single-pixel dithering, drawn at 16px per
tile. On modern hi-res displays the game was only usable at UI scale
200-300% (`Options > UI Scale`, which sets `sun.java2d.uiScale`), and
that scaling blew the 16px bitmaps up into visible blocks.

Two complementary fixes were made (June 2026):

* *Bigger, crisper map graphics.* A 64x64 zoom level was added (zoom
  range is now 8-64), and the map painter is HiDPI-aware: it picks the
  tile sheet at the smallest available size covering
  `zoom x device scale`, so at UI scale 2-3x the map is rendered from
  genuinely higher-resolution art instead of upscaled bitmaps.
* *Modern full-color art.* Land, water, forest, parks, rubble, flood,
  radiation, scorch and fire tiles, plus roads, rails, power wires,
  bridges and traffic, are now procedurally drawn in full color without
  dithering. Building and zone sheets are run through a de-dithering
  filter that blends the dense two-color checkerboards into smooth
  shades (window grids and other sparse pixel detail stay crisp), then
  upscaled with Scale2x/Scale4x for the 32px and 64px sheets until they
  get real redrawn art (see TODO).

The original art remains available in-game via *Options > Graphics >
Classic* (preference `tile_skin`, see `TileSkin`) and is the default
skin; the switch applies live, no restart needed. Classic sheets live under
`src/main/resources/classic/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}/` and are composed
from the base 16px sources only (no `_NxN` variants), reproducing the
pre-modernization look — including the manually darkened park tiles,
which were ported from the old composed sheets back into
`graphics/terrain.png` / `graphics/misc_animation.png`.

== Pipeline

Source art lives in `graphics/`; the game loads pre-composed sheets
from `src/main/resources/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64,sm}/tiles.png(+.idx)`.
The sheets are static checked-in resources; regenerate them with:

----
graphics/build-tiles.sh
----

The script:

1. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.DeDither` on the building/zone sheets
   (zones, civic buildings, their animation frames), producing cleaned
   `name_16x16.png` variants;
2. derives `name_32x32.png` / `name_64x64.png` source sheets from the
   16px art via `micropolisj.build_tool.UpscaleArt` (Scale2x/EPX) —
   preferring the de-dithered `_16x16` when one exists — and
   `name_8x8.png` via `micropolisj.build_tool.HalveArt` (box filter);
3. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateTerrainArt`, which overwrites
   the terrain and fountain sheets with procedural art
   (`terrain_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`,
   `misc_animation_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`);
4. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateOverlayArt`, which draws the
   road, rail, wire, rail+road and traffic sheets
   (`{roads,rails,wires,roadwire,traffic_frames}_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`);
5. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateDeluxeArt`, which produces the
   deluxe skin's building sheets
   (`graphics/deluxe/gen/*_{64x64,32x32}.png`, gitignored);
6. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.MakeTiles` for tile sizes 3 (minimap),
   8, 16, 32 and 64, plus a second "classic" pass that stages only the
   base 16px sources into a temp dir and composes
   `src/main/resources/classic/{N}x{N}`, and a third "deluxe" pass that
   stages the modern sources, overlays the generated
   `graphics/deluxe/gen/` art and then any committed hand-made
   `graphics/deluxe/` redraws (deriving the latter's 32/16/8px variants
   by box-filter halving) and composes
   `src/main/resources/deluxe/{N}x{N}`;
7. keeps `src/main/resources/tiles.rc` in sync with `graphics/tiles.rc`;
8. derives `obj*_32x32.png` / `obj*_64x64.png` sprite variants;
9. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateToolIcons`, which draws the
   toolbar tool icons as stylized glyphs for the modern and deluxe skins
   (`src/main/resources/tools/ic*.png` and
   `src/main/resources/deluxe/tools/ic*.png`). The classic skin keeps the
   original root `ic*.png`; the GUI picks the skinned variant per the
   active `TileSkin` and refreshes the toolbar when the theme changes.

The derived `graphics/*_NxN.png` intermediates are gitignored; only the
composed sheets in `src/main/resources/` are committed.

MakeTiles resolves source art per size: `name_NxN.svg`, `name.svg`
(rendered with Inkscape), `name_NxN.png`, `name_128x128.png`, then
`name.png` (16px basis, scaled). Dropping a higher-resolution source
next to the 16px original is enough — no build-tool changes needed.

=== Procedural terrain art

`GenerateTerrainArt` redraws tiles 0-1 (dirt), 2-20 (water and river
edges), 21-37 (trees/forest edges), 40-43 (park woods), 44-47 (rubble),
48-51 (flood), 52 (radiation), 53-55 (scorched ground) and 56-63
(fire animation), plus the four fountain animation frames (tile 840)
inside `misc_animation`.

The original 16px tiles are used as *material masks*: each pixel is
classified as water / tree / land by palette color, the masks are
upsampled and smoothed, and procedural textures are rendered through
them (noise-mottled dirt, water with depth and wobbled shorelines,
individually lit tree crowns). This reproduces every shoreline and
forest-edge orientation of the original tile set automatically.
Park tiles (40-43), rubble, flood, radiation, scorch and fire are
drawn from scratch. Average land/water tones stay close to the
original palette so the redrawn terrain doesn't clash at seams with
the still-upscaled building tiles.

=== Procedural road/rail/wire art

`GenerateOverlayArt` redraws the transport and power overlays:

* *Roads* (`roads.png`): drawn geometrically from per-row path layouts
  (straights, quarter arcs for curves, T- and X-junctions) — asphalt
  band with a dark outline and dashed center line; the bridge rows
  (incl. the open drawbridge) are geometric too. The same surface is
  used for the road part of the rail crossings.
* *Rails* (`rails.png`): drawn geometrically from per-row track layouts
  (straights, curves, T- and X-junctions, rail+road crossings) —
  gravel ballast, wooden ties, twin steel rails. Underwater rows are a
  dashed "tunnel" ghost so they stay visible on the new water.
* *Wires* (`wires.png`, `roadwire.png`): poles with caps and the twin
  hanging cables of the original art; the rail+wire crossings draw the
  same twin cables geometrically at matching positions. Underwater rows
  are a dashed submarine-cable band.
* *Traffic* (`traffic_frames.png`): the redrawn road is the base layer
  (traffic tiles are layered composites in `tiles.rc`); the car
  animation is synthesized along geometric lanes offset from the road
  centerline (so curve lanes meet straight lanes exactly at tile
  seams). Cars advance a quarter lane-length per frame, making the
  4-frame loop seamless; colors are keyed to the car's identity, not
  its position, so they stay stable across frames, and drawing is
  clipped per cell so cars can't bleed into neighboring frames.

=== Deluxe building art

`GenerateDeluxeArt` produces the deluxe skin's building/zone sheets from
the de-dithered 16px art:

* a pre-blend pass melts the dense two-color checkerboards that
  survived `DeDither`'s conservative pass (narrow dithered bands such
  as awnings/roof trim) into their blended shade — same
  alternation-candidate detection, but blending wherever the 3x3
  candidate density reaches 6, which a true checkerboard always does
  (9) and a sparse window grid never does (<=5);
* an edge-aware mean-shift 4x upscale: each output pixel starts from a
  bilinear estimate of its source neighborhood and is pulled toward the
  locally dominant color mode by a joint spatial/range kernel
  (premultiplied alpha, so transparent smoke frames don't halo). Flat
  shades become smooth gradients, staircase diagonals become
  anti-aliased curves, and sparse 1px detail survives because the range
  kernel keeps distinct colors apart;
* a finishing pass adds a gentle saturation lift and a subtle material
  grain (periodic per tile so map neighbors join seamlessly);
* the fountain frames inside `misc_animation` are pasted back from the
  procedural art so the deluxe skin keeps them.

Only `misc_animation` still goes through the upscaler, as the base
sheet `DeluxeMiscArt` patches its redrawn tiles into: the power-out
indicator, the eight raised-drawbridge tiles (deck geometry and palette
matching the procedural road bridges), the eight-frame zone-destruction
explosion, the airport radar sweep (from `DeluxePortArt`) and the
nuclear reactor swirl (from `DeluxePowerArt`); the unused tiles keep
the upscaled art, and at 16px/8px the redrawn regions are pasted over
the modern de-dithered sheet.

Every building sheet is redrawn completely from scratch (each batch
graduated out of the upscaler after being validated in-game):
`DeluxeResArt` draws the residential sheets (`res_zones`,
`res_houses`) procedurally at 64px per tile — empty zone, 16 apartment
blocks (4 land values x 4 densities), hospital, church and the 12
single-family houses — keeping the classic tile language (green hedge
border, green "R" sign, dirt-to-lawn value progression, gray slabs ->
blue roofs -> awning-trimmed brick -> white luxury with the twin round
towers, the hospital's red roof cross, the church steeple; sun from the
northwest like the terrain art). `DeluxeComArt` draws the commercial
sheet (`com_zones`) the same way — empty zone plus 20 business blocks
(4 land values x 5 densities) with a concrete sidewalk ring instead of
the hedge, the blue "C" sign, storefront rows with colored sign bands
and display windows growing into glass curtain-wall towers, and the
classic landmarks: the dense red-and-blue towers, the striped office
tower, a gas station, the stepped ziggurats and the grand white
cylinder tower at top value. `DeluxeIndArt` draws the industrial
sheets (`ind_zones` plus the `ind01_pistons_frames` and
`ind03/04/07/08_smoke_frames` animation overlays) — empty zone plus 8
factory blocks (2 land values x 4 densities) with the yellow
safety-line ring instead of hedge/sidewalk, the yellow "I" sign, dirt
lots at every value, gray corrugated halls and the collar-banded
smokestacks (vertical, in the same oblique projection as the church
bell tower); landmarks kept include the piston pump bay,
the ore yard, the sphere-tank chemical depot and the round riveted gas
holder. The chimney mouth positions are shared constants between the
block renderer and the animation renderer, so the 4-frame smoke plumes
rise exactly off the stacks and stay inside the tiles `tiles.rc`
composites them over, and the 8 piston frames are cut from the same
block render so the powered/unpowered swap is pixel-stable.

The civic and special buildings follow the same playbook, each keeping
its classic ring color and identity mark. `DeluxeCivicArt` draws the
fire station (red ring, three engine bays onto a concrete apron, the
rooftop "FD", the hose tower and an engine rolling out) and the police
station (blue ring, rooftop "PD", columned entrance with the blue lamp,
cruiser lot), and hosts the shared kit: the parameterized ring border
and the rooftop letters. Both stations carry a beacon-light animation —
the fire alarm lamp pulsing red, the police rooftop pair wig-wagging
red and blue — added as new always-on animations: `tiles.rc` composites
`firestation_light_animation` / `police_lights_animation` over tiles
763 and 771, the lamp fixtures and the glow frames share position
constants, and the 16px base frame sheets are generated into
`graphics/` (gitignored) so the classic and modern skins compose the
same frames from their own art. `DeluxeStadiumArt` draws the empty and full
stadium bowls (green ring, oval terraced stands, striped pitch) plus
the eight football-game frames; the frame background is cropped from
the very same full-bowl render, so the animated column — tiles (2,1)
and (2,2), where `MapScanner` composites `FOOTBALLGAME1/2` — stays
pixel-identical to its static neighbors, and player motion is
sinusoidal in the frame counter so the loop is seamless.
`DeluxePowerArt` draws the coal plant (coal yard and conveyor, boiler
house, turbine hall, transformer yard, and four collar-banded stacks up
the northeast diagonal whose `coal_smoke_frames` reuse the industrial
plume renderer over shared stack-mouth constants) and the nuclear plant
(the two huge cooling-tower mouths seen straight from above, the white
containment sphere, the navy roof atom, and the fenced reactor pad the
4-frame swirl animation lights up — three orbits 60 degrees apart
turning 15 degrees per frame, so the loop closes). `DeluxePortArt`
draws the seaport (blue ring, the navy anchor on the cargo hall roof,
container yard, gantry crane) and the airport (crossing runways 9/27
and 18/36 with thresholds and numbers, terminal with jetway gates and
parked airliners, control tower, hangar, fuel farm, windsock; the
radar frames crop the block's own tile (2,0) ground so the 8-frame
dish sweep sits seamlessly on the redrawn airport). Redrawn sheets
emit all four sizes by downscaling, like `GenerateTerrainArt`.

=== De-dithered buildings

`DeDither` removes the dense two-color dithering the 80s art uses to
fake intermediate shades: a pixel is an _alternation candidate_ when
at least 3 of its 4 neighbours differ and agree on one partner color;
where candidates are dense (a true checkerboard, not a sparse window
grid) every pixel is replaced by its local 3x3 average. Applied to:
airport, coal, com_zones, firestation, ind_zones, misc_animation,
nuclear, police, res_houses, res_zones, seaport, stadium sheets and
their animation-frame sheets.

== Deluxe skin

A third skin, *Deluxe* (infrastructure implemented June 2026), joins
Classic and Modern: every tile completely redrawn as full-color art,
replacing the de-dithered Scale2x/Scale4x upscales that Modern still
uses for buildings and zones. What is in place:

* *`TileSkin`*: third constant `DELUXE = "deluxe"`; `resourcePrefix`
  returns `"deluxe/"` (modern keeps the empty prefix). The menu order
  and default are Classic (default), Modern, Deluxe. The Options >
  Graphics radio item comes for free — `MainWindow` builds the menu
  from `TileSkin.getSupportedValues()` — and the live-switch path
  (`TileSkin.setPreference` + `refreshTileImages`) needed no changes.
* *i18n*: `menu.options.graphics.deluxe` in all five
  `GuiStrings*.properties` bundles (en/de/fr/it/sv); "Deluxe" is left
  untranslated in every locale.
* *Source art*: the `graphics/deluxe/` directory (see its README)
  holds the redrawn sheets at 64px basis (`name_64x64.png`, same sheet
  layout and row order as the base art so `tiles.rc` and the `.ani`
  files work unchanged). Sheets not yet redrawn simply aren't present —
  they fall back to the modern art, so the skin stays usable while the
  redraw is incomplete.
* *`build-tiles.sh`*: a third MakeTiles pass stages the modern sources
  (base art + generated/derived `_NxN` files) into a temp dir — the
  same staging trick the classic pass uses — copies the
  `graphics/deluxe/` overrides on top, derives each override's
  32/16/8px variants by box-filter halving (unless explicit `_NxN`
  files are committed in `graphics/deluxe/`), and composes
  `src/main/resources/deluxe/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}/`. The minimap
  (`sm/`) stays shared across skins.

The skin ships with generated art: `GenerateDeluxeArt` (see the
pipeline notes above) replaces the blocky Scale2x/Scale4x building
upscales with smooth, anti-aliased full-color renderings at the 32px
and 64px zoom levels. True hand redraws (the TODO list below) remain
welcome — committed art in `graphics/deluxe/` always wins over the
generated sheets.

== Prior manual art work (do not regress)

* Commit `772212c` — the 16 toolbar icons (`src/main/resources/ic*.png`)
  were manually de-dithered. They are standalone resources, untouched by
  the regen script; future icon work must not revert them.
* Commit `1096377` — park tiles were manually darkened to distinguish
  parks from forest. Those pixels were originally edited directly in the
  composed sheets; they have since been ported back into the source art
  (`graphics/terrain.png` rows 40-43, `graphics/misc_animation.png`
  fountain frames), so the classic skin reproduces them. In the modern
  skin the parks are procedural, and the *intent* is preserved as a
  design rule: *park trees must stay clearly darker and visually
  distinct from forest trees at every tile size*.

Notes for whoever picks these up:

* Provide redrawn art as `graphics/deluxe/name_64x64.png` (64px basis
  per tile) and commit it; `build-tiles.sh` derives the smaller sizes
  by halving automatically. (Hand-made improvements to the
  *modern* skin instead go next to the base art as
  `graphics/name_NxN.png`: `build-tiles.sh` skips deriving any `_NxN`
  file that is tracked by git, so committed hand-made art is never
  overwritten — procedural art doesn't need committing, its generator
  runs after the derive step and overwrites the derived files.)
* Keep tile color averages close to the original palette where redrawn
  tiles meet still-upscaled neighbours.
* Animations: frame coordinates in `.ani` files are in 16px-basis
  units; `_NxN` sources are picked up automatically.
