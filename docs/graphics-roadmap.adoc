= Graphics Roadmap
:toc:

Modernization of the SimpliCity graphics: motivation, how the pipeline
works, what has been done, and what is still TODO.

== Motivation

The original MicropolisJ art dates back to 1980s hardware limits: a
~14-color palette with heavy single-pixel dithering, drawn at 16px per
tile. On modern hi-res displays the game was only usable at UI scale
200-300% (`Options > UI Scale`, which sets `sun.java2d.uiScale`), and
that scaling blew the 16px bitmaps up into visible blocks.

Two complementary fixes were made (June 2026):

* *Bigger, crisper map graphics.* A 64x64 zoom level was added (zoom
  range is now 8-64), and the map painter is HiDPI-aware: it picks the
  tile sheet at the smallest available size covering
  `zoom x device scale`, so at UI scale 2-3x the map is rendered from
  genuinely higher-resolution art instead of upscaled bitmaps.
* *Modern full-color art.* Land, water, forest, parks, rubble, flood,
  radiation, scorch and fire tiles, plus roads, rails, power wires,
  bridges and traffic, are now procedurally drawn in full color without
  dithering. Building and zone sheets are run through a de-dithering
  filter that blends the dense two-color checkerboards into smooth
  shades (window grids and other sparse pixel detail stay crisp), then
  upscaled with Scale2x/Scale4x for the 32px and 64px sheets until they
  get real redrawn art (see TODO).

The original art remains available in-game via *Options > Graphics >
Classic* (preference `tile_skin`, see `TileSkin`); the switch applies
live, no restart needed. Classic sheets live under
`src/main/resources/classic/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}/` and are composed
from the base 16px sources only (no `_NxN` variants), reproducing the
pre-modernization look — including the manually darkened park tiles,
which were ported from the old composed sheets back into
`graphics/terrain.png` / `graphics/misc_animation.png`.

== Pipeline

Source art lives in `graphics/`; the game loads pre-composed sheets
from `src/main/resources/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64,sm}/tiles.png(+.idx)`.
The sheets are static checked-in resources; regenerate them with:

----
graphics/build-tiles.sh
----

The script:

1. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.DeDither` on the building/zone sheets
   (zones, civic buildings, their animation frames), producing cleaned
   `name_16x16.png` variants;
2. derives `name_32x32.png` / `name_64x64.png` source sheets from the
   16px art via `micropolisj.build_tool.UpscaleArt` (Scale2x/EPX) —
   preferring the de-dithered `_16x16` when one exists — and
   `name_8x8.png` via `micropolisj.build_tool.HalveArt` (box filter);
3. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateTerrainArt`, which overwrites
   the terrain and fountain sheets with procedural art
   (`terrain_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`,
   `misc_animation_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`);
4. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.GenerateOverlayArt`, which draws the
   road, rail, wire, rail+road and traffic sheets
   (`{roads,rails,wires,roadwire,traffic_frames}_{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}.png`);
5. runs `micropolisj.build_tool.MakeTiles` for tile sizes 3 (minimap),
   8, 16, 32 and 64, plus a second "classic" pass that stages only the
   base 16px sources into a temp dir and composes
   `src/main/resources/classic/{N}x{N}`;
6. keeps `src/main/resources/tiles.rc` in sync with `graphics/tiles.rc`;
7. derives `obj*_32x32.png` / `obj*_64x64.png` sprite variants.

The derived `graphics/*_NxN.png` intermediates are gitignored; only the
composed sheets in `src/main/resources/` are committed.

MakeTiles resolves source art per size: `name_NxN.svg`, `name.svg`
(rendered with Inkscape), `name_NxN.png`, `name_128x128.png`, then
`name.png` (16px basis, scaled). Dropping a higher-resolution source
next to the 16px original is enough — no build-tool changes needed.

=== Procedural terrain art

`GenerateTerrainArt` redraws tiles 0-1 (dirt), 2-20 (water and river
edges), 21-37 (trees/forest edges), 40-43 (park woods), 44-47 (rubble),
48-51 (flood), 52 (radiation), 53-55 (scorched ground) and 56-63
(fire animation), plus the four fountain animation frames (tile 840)
inside `misc_animation`.

The original 16px tiles are used as *material masks*: each pixel is
classified as water / tree / land by palette color, the masks are
upsampled and smoothed, and procedural textures are rendered through
them (noise-mottled dirt, water with depth and wobbled shorelines,
individually lit tree crowns). This reproduces every shoreline and
forest-edge orientation of the original tile set automatically.
Park tiles (40-43), rubble, flood, radiation, scorch and fire are
drawn from scratch. Average land/water tones stay close to the
original palette so the redrawn terrain doesn't clash at seams with
the still-upscaled building tiles.

=== Procedural road/rail/wire art

`GenerateOverlayArt` redraws the transport and power overlays:

* *Roads* (`roads.png`): drawn geometrically from per-row path layouts
  (straights, quarter arcs for curves, T- and X-junctions) — asphalt
  band with a dark outline and dashed center line; the bridge rows
  (incl. the open drawbridge) are geometric too. The same surface is
  used for the road part of the rail crossings.
* *Rails* (`rails.png`): drawn geometrically from per-row track layouts
  (straights, curves, T- and X-junctions, rail+road crossings) —
  gravel ballast, wooden ties, twin steel rails. Underwater rows are a
  dashed "tunnel" ghost so they stay visible on the new water.
* *Wires* (`wires.png`, `roadwire.png`): poles with caps and the twin
  hanging cables of the original art; the rail+wire crossings draw the
  same twin cables geometrically at matching positions. Underwater rows
  are a dashed submarine-cable band.
* *Traffic* (`traffic_frames.png`): the redrawn road is the base layer
  (traffic tiles are layered composites in `tiles.rc`); the car
  animation is synthesized along geometric lanes offset from the road
  centerline (so curve lanes meet straight lanes exactly at tile
  seams). Cars advance a quarter lane-length per frame, making the
  4-frame loop seamless; colors are keyed to the car's identity, not
  its position, so they stay stable across frames, and drawing is
  clipped per cell so cars can't bleed into neighboring frames.

=== De-dithered buildings

`DeDither` removes the dense two-color dithering the 80s art uses to
fake intermediate shades: a pixel is an _alternation candidate_ when
at least 3 of its 4 neighbours differ and agree on one partner color;
where candidates are dense (a true checkerboard, not a sparse window
grid) every pixel is replaced by its local 3x3 average. Applied to:
airport, coal, com_zones, firestation, ind_zones, misc_animation,
nuclear, police, res_houses, res_zones, seaport, stadium sheets and
their animation-frame sheets.

== Deluxe skin (planned)

A third skin, *Deluxe*, will join Classic and Modern: every tile
completely redrawn as full-color art, replacing the de-dithered
Scale2x/Scale4x upscales that Modern still uses for buildings and
zones. Implementation steps:

1. *`TileSkin`*: add a third constant `DELUXE = "deluxe"` next to
   `MODERN`/`CLASSIC`; `resourcePrefix` returns `"deluxe/"` for it.
   Modern stays the default (empty prefix), so existing preferences
   keep working.
2. *`MainWindow`*: a third radio item in Options > Graphics, wired
   through the existing `graphicsMenuItems` /
   `onGraphicsSkinClicked` machinery — the live-switch path
   (`TileSkin.setPreference` + `refreshTileImages`) needs no changes.
3. *i18n*: add `menu.options.graphics.deluxe` to all five
   `GuiStrings*.properties` bundles (en/de/fr/it/sv); "Deluxe" works
   untranslated in every locale.
4. *Source art*: a dedicated `graphics/deluxe/` directory holding the
   redrawn sheets at 64px basis (`name_64x64.png`, same sheet layout
   and row order as the base art so `tiles.rc` and the `.ani` files
   work unchanged). Sheets not yet redrawn simply aren't present —
   they fall back to the modern art, so the skin stays usable while
   the redraw is incomplete.
5. *`build-tiles.sh`*: a third MakeTiles pass that stages the modern
   sources (base art + generated/derived `_NxN` files) into a temp
   dir — the same staging trick the classic pass uses — then copies
   the `graphics/deluxe/` overrides on top, and composes
   `src/main/resources/deluxe/{8x8,16x16,32x32,64x64}/`. The minimap
   (`sm/`) stays shared across skins.
6. *The art itself*: the redraw workload in the TODO list below.

== Prior manual art work (do not regress)

* Commit `772212c` — the 16 toolbar icons (`src/main/resources/ic*.png`)
  were manually de-dithered. They are standalone resources, untouched by
  the regen script; future icon work must not revert them.
* Commit `1096377` — park tiles were manually darkened to distinguish
  parks from forest. Those pixels were originally edited directly in the
  composed sheets; they have since been ported back into the source art
  (`graphics/terrain.png` rows 40-43, `graphics/misc_animation.png`
  fountain frames), so the classic skin reproduces them. In the modern
  skin the parks are procedural, and the *intent* is preserved as a
  design rule: *park trees must stay clearly darker and visually
  distinct from forest trees at every tile size*.

Notes for whoever picks these up:

* Provide redrawn art as `graphics/deluxe/name_64x64.png` (64px basis
  per tile) and commit it; smaller sizes can be derived by downscaling,
  like `GenerateTerrainArt` does. (Hand-made improvements to the
  *modern* skin instead go next to the base art as
  `graphics/name_NxN.png`: `build-tiles.sh` skips deriving any `_NxN`
  file that is tracked by git, so committed hand-made art is never
  overwritten — procedural art doesn't need committing, its generator
  runs after the derive step and overwrites the derived files.)
* Keep tile color averages close to the original palette where redrawn
  tiles meet still-upscaled neighbours.
* Animations: frame coordinates in `.ani` files are in 16px-basis
  units; `_NxN` sources are picked up automatically.
